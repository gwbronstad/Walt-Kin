A studio (Probably Hico Texas) picture of Gulrand and Laura's growing fammily. Looks to be about 1903 or 1904; the youngest, Lorine Galinda (Bronstad) Hudson was born 1902-07-30

Probably a school picture. Clyde and Marie would have been about 3 year grades apart.

The picture is dated 1911; Clyde would have been 14 or 15, Otis 16 or 17. Immigrants and their childrwen would have been anxious to assimilate. What better than joining a national sport.

Maybe a Lutheran Church confirmation picture?

Clyde entered The Great War too late to get a free ride to France; he did get the Spanish Flu however. He trained for the Balloon corps. Before aircraft could communicate reliably to the ground, the U S Army followed the French example of tethered balloons to grant an elevated view of the battlefield, helping direct artillery fire.

Clyde strikes a jaunty pose in center. To the left is Chris Rohne holding daughter LoVerne, making this picture about 1924.

Clyde and Louise on an outing in the hill country, Bob Holbrook is squatting in the right quarter of the picture.

Maybe it's 1940 and Clyde is holding an infant Gilbert Walton Bronstad beside the house Clyde built in Cranfills Gap for his new bride.

Clyde, and Louise sitting in front of their 4710 E Loraine home with their grandson, Mark Holbrook. Clyde and Louise lived there since 1954. Mark looks about 5 or 6 makiing this photo about 1969.

Behind Clyde is the 1957 home addition put on by one of the Bertelson's from Cranfills Gap.

It's 1969, Clyde and Louise visited Walt and Pat in their Longshore Drive apartment in Ann Arbor.

Clyde's upper lip is distorted by a cyst he had removed at a later date.

Portrait possibly for a church position in his Lutheran Church.

The tan curtains suggest the Loraine address.

Clyde frequently read the Fort Worth Star Telegram, Popular Mechanics, etc.

This looks to be from late 1970's or early 1980's.

Clyde's hands were the large, big-boned hands of a working man, here shown even larger with arthritic swelling. I loved the contrasts of their hands.

A series of hip fractures left Clyde with limited mobility. Louise and Bette got Clyde a special chair to ease transitions.

Clyde was not willing to lose mobility entirely. He stayed with my family in Austin for a few days.
