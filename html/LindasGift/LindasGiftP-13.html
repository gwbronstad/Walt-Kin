"George has not come yet from town," Mother told Father as they came into the house.

"It is taking longer to fix the car than we thought," Father replied.

A little fear caught at Llnda's mind. What if George didn't get back in time to finish the plowing?" But surely Father would ask Mamie to plow if George didn't come.

 
  
 (Mamie) 
  
 

Mother had always told Linda to rest after lunch if she had been working in the field. So she lay across the bed.

As she lay there she thought of the things she would do in just a few minutes. She would take a bath, wash her hair, and let it dry quickly in the sun.

 
  
 (claw foot tub) 
  
 

Then she would put on one of her best dresses and slip a pretty blue ribbon in her hair. After helping Mother wash dishes, she would make some lemonade.

 
  
 (pitcher of lemonade) 
  
 

All these plans were running through her mind when sleep overtook her.
