The sun grew hotter and hotter, and Linda's face and jacket were damp with perspiration. Soon she saw Father stop near a tree where the jug of water had been left.

"Come geet a cool drink, Linda," Father called.

To stop the horses, she pulled on the reins and tied them to the lever. Reaching the tree, she found the water refreshing but not cold.

Sand filled her shoes as she walked back to the plow. Her toes were bent under by the crowding sand. After she climbed on the plow, she removed each shoe ahd shook out the sand.
