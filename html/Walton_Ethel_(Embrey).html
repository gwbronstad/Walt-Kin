Mark 001



Ethel's Fatther, James Monroe Embrey was from a long line of mobile southern farmers ( Tippah, MS 🠤 Oglethorp, GA 🠤 Madison, KY.)


Ethel's Mother, Josephine Cameron or Camburn was of a moderately long line of Georgia farmers ( Rome, GA 🠤 GA 🠤 Scotland.).


James Monroe Embrey was a Confederate veteran. Both he and Josephine Cameron/Camburn survived the destructive, disruptive "War between the States". In the aftermath of the war each was a refugee from their homes and families.


Family stories describe how the Yankees (During Sherman's Atlanta Campaign) took all the food at the Cameron/Camburn, Rome Georgia farm, killed all the chickens, even stripped the unripe peaches from the trees. Some malnourished children in the family died of childhood disease.


Tossed by war's destructions and misfortunes, it is to their credit that James and Josephine could rebuild lives in the west (Central Texas).


The 1880 U S census shows two other daughters, Eddie and Maud, senior to Ethel Irene. Those girls fill in the gap between Paulene and Ethel. But the census information is hard to accept. James Monroe's occupation is listed as "Physician". Was he using field bandaging skills gained in the Confederate Army? The wife is Ida, not Josephine, was Ida her unrecorded middle name, or a pet name of James? James and and Ida's ages seem to match. But what has happened to Mary Pauline? She's not in the census.


I think this is the picture of the ambiguous status of "Aunt Pauline." She appears to have struggled from becomming a "non-person". This may be at the heart of William Twyman's reputed low regard for Mother-in-law, Josephine.


The added LDS Family search data show Aunt Pauline wed a MR Daniel Riley McFatter 1895-11-13 in Hamilton, TX. They had 3 children. I faintly recall grandmaother Ethel Walton speaking of the Mc Fatters with my Mother, Louise. The Burger name suggests that one Augustus H Berger was Mary Pauline Embrey's biological father. Missouri records show that Josephine Cambern wed Mr Berger 1870 in Jackson, Missouri.


So what happened to grandmother Ethel's sisters Eddie and Maud? The 1990 UK S Census was lost to fire, Childhood diseases took a toll.
